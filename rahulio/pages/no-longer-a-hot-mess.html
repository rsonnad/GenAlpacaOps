An email from a stranger · February 10, 2015

“No longer a hot mess.”

Years earlier, a woman ran out of gas on the west side of LA. A stranger helped and handed her an Eddie Vedder mix CD. This is the email she sent years later.

L
Luelle Llorens
to crazyveddie@gmail.com
Feb 10, 2015 · 7:01 AM

Hey there,

You helped me out once when my car ran out of gas on the West side of LA.

It was highly embarrassing, and you were very kind and supportive.

You gave me a mixed CD of Eddie Vedder songs.

I've moved across the country and have been carrying that thing around with me for years. Saying I'd write you one day and thank you for your kindness. And finally let you know that I got my shit together.

Well.

Today's that day.

“You're a hot mess…”

— What you said to me that day

I want to say that I really appreciated you calling me out.

Pretty sure my account was overdrawn too. And you got to witness my credit card being declined.

It was refreshing to have someone call me out on the bullshit. Lol

It's been years since this happened.

Not sure if you even recall the day or moment.

But I haven't forgotten the feeling of that encounter.

“Here's a CD for when you're down…”

It was weird, but loving all at once.
And from a complete stranger.

Very refreshing.

Since that day close to Marina Del Rey, I have moved to Florida and I'm in my 3rd year of teaching English to Middle schoolers.

I freaking love it.

And tomorrow I might just pop your CD in my car and listen to it once more while smiling knowing, everything's gonna be all right.

Thanks again. Keep being you.

Sincerely,
Luelle Llorens
