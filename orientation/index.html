Orientation Guide

Everything you need to know to enjoy your time at the Playhouse

Principles

Throughout history, successful co-living communities have been built on the principle that the interests of the individual are enhanced by the condition of the commons. Each of us at the Austin Alpaca Playhouse is a steward of the commons. We each contribute to enhance our shared environment through mindful attention to shared resources, invitations, and community gatherings.

As a member of the Alpaca community we hope you resonate with the following principles:

  • Respect the Commons
  • Contribute
  • Embody
  • Gratitude
  • Wisdom

Things you should know

  • NO MEAT inside – You can put meat in the doghouse refrigerator (not the garage), and you can cook meat outside, but don't stare at the Alpaca when you do.
  • NO ALCOHOL (unless it's your wedding)
  • Clean up after yourself – Don't leave your bags or any items on counters in the Living Room or Kitchen. Better to stash in the dining room. Don't leave beverage cans, food items, tea cups, or any random stuff in the living room area.
  • Toilets – Don't put anything in toilets other than toilet paper or you will wreck things.

Parking

Please follow signs and Respect Neighborhood Sovereignty.

  • DO NOT park in front of neighbors houses.
  • DO NOT park on the grass.
  • When you come in the gate, you can park in the gravel lot on the right when you come in, if it is not full already.
  • If there is no space in the lot you can park across the street to the left past two houses, facing the highway.
  • DO NOT PARK OR DRIVE ANYWHERE ON THE GRASS beyond the gate. Sprinklers may get damaged, and you're likely to get stuck if it has rained.
  • Don't ever park your car on the circle or by the garage. This obstructs Amazon delivery access, which is fundamental.

Music

Play great music! Please turn amazing music on using Spotify device connect or the "Sonos S1" app with the secret network on the Black Rock City wifi.

Be careful – make sure you are playing in the right "room" on Sonos, vs. blasting out some innocent sleeping bystander in a different room. Make sure just your room is connected in most cases. Too many have lost sleep and hearing…

You need to be on Black Rock City wifi for Sonos to work.

Alpaca Care

Always, always, always keep the back fence gates closed. Don't leave them open for a second or the Alpaca will wander off, and you may spend the rest of your life chasing lost alpaca dreams.

DO NOT call the Alpaca "Llamas". They look so sweet, but if they hear you call them "Llama", they will likely pull a knife and take you out – quick and silent and drag you behind the barn.

Dogs: Don't let your dog enter the Alpaca domain in the back, even though they are so special that one could only assume the rules surely wouldn't apply to such a divine & gracious creature. You can tie them in the front.
